After graduating from Haverford College in May 2015, I've immersed myself in all aspects of web development and design. Starting out with a background in English, I began my adventures into the great world wide web through the field of digital scholarship — creating websites which analyzed data drawn from rare, Quaker manuscripts.

It was during this internship in digital scholarship and through the process of creating my first website, that I realized I had a genuine passion for web development like nothing else I'd ever done, or studied. As a web developer now, I pride myself on making functional, beautiful, and accessible websites which provide a useful and thought-provoking experience for users.

In my free time, I enjoy walking around Pittsburgh when it’s sunny, eating chocolate chip cookies, playing a bit of frisbee, and watching the Great British Bake Off.
